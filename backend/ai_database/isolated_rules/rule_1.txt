

##1. CORE FORMULAS & MATHEMATICS  
- **Ohm's Law**: $ V = IR $ (Voltage = Current × Resistance)  
- **Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)**: $ \sum V = 0 $ (Sum of voltages in a loop = 0)  
- **Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)**: $ \sum I = 0 $ (Sum of currents at a node = 0)  
- **Power Dissipation**: $ P = VI = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R} $  
- **Impedance in AC Circuits**: $ Z = R + jX $ (Resistance + jReactance)  
- **Thevenin Equivalent**: $ V_{th} = V_{open}, R_{th} = R_{source} $  
- **Norton Equivalent**: $ I_{n} = I_{short}, R_{n} = R_{source} $  
- **Resonance Frequency (RLC)**: $ f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}} $  
- **Capacitive Reactance**: $ X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi fC} $  
- **Inductive Reactance**: $ X_L = 2\pi fL $  
- **Voltage Divider**: $ V_{out} = V_{in} \cdot \frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2} $  
- **Current Divider**: $ I_{out} = I_{in} \cdot \frac{R_1}{R_1 + R_2} $

## 2. DESIGN ORDER OF OPERATIONS  
1. Define circuit purpose (e.g., filter, voltage divider, impedance matching).  
2. Calculate required parameters (e.g., cutoff frequency, resistance values).  
3. Select components based on calculated values and standard tolerances.  
4. Simulate or analyze using KVL/KCL or nodal analysis.  
5. Build and test on breadboard, verify with multimeter.  

---

## 3. STANDARD VALUES & PARAMETERS  
- **Supply Voltages**: 5V, 12V, 24V (common in labs).  
- **Resistor Values**: 1kΩ, 10kΩ, 100kΩ (E24 series).  
- **Capacitor Values**: 100nF, 1μF, 10μF (ceramic/ electrolytic).  
- **Inductor Values**: 100μH, 1mH (air-core).  
- **Operating Ranges**: Resistors ±5%, capacitors ±20%.  
- **Thresholds**: Breakdown voltage for diodes (e.g., 0.7V for Si).  

---

## 4. PRACTICAL COMPONENT SELECTION RULES  
- **Capacitors**: Use ceramic for high-frequency stability, electrolytic for high capacitance.  
- **Resistors**: 1/4W for low-power circuits, 1W for high-current paths.  
- **Inductors**: Air-core for RF, ferrite for low-frequency.  
- **ICs**: 555 timer for oscillators, op-amps for amplifiers.  

---

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES & TROUBLESHOOTING  
1. **Incorrect Component Values**: Symptom: Circuit not functioning. Action: Recalculate using formulas.  
2. **Mixing Series/Parallel**: Symptom: Unexpected current/voltage. Action: Redraw circuit diagram.  
3. **Ignoring Power Ratings**: Symptom: Overheating. Action: Replace with higher-rated components.  
4. **Poor Soldering**: Symptom: Intermittent connections. Action: Re-solder joints.  
5. **Not Checking Continuity**: Symptom: No output. Action: Use multimeter to verify connections.  

---

## 6. VIVA & INTERVIEW TRAPS  
1. **Question**: "Explain the difference between impedance and resistance in AC circuits."  
   **Answer**: Impedance includes reactance (capacitive/inductive), while resistance is real.  
2. **Question**: "Why is resonance important in RLC circuits?"  
   **Answer**: Maximizes current/voltage at a specific frequency.  
3. **Question**: "What is the purpose of a voltage divider?"  
   **Answer**: To scale down voltage levels proportionally.  
4. **Question**: "How does temperature affect resistor values?"  
   **Answer**: Resistance changes with temperature (positive/negative temperature coefficient).  
5. **Question**: "Why use a Thevenin equivalent?"  
   **Answer**: Simplifies complex networks for analysis.
