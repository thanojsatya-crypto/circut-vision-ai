## 2. DESIGN ORDER OF OPERATIONS

1. **Define performance specifications**  
   - Bandwidth, gain, phase margin, noise, power, size.  
2. **Select system type** (continuous‑time, discrete‑time, analog filter, digital filter).  
3. **Choose basic topology** (e.g., Sallen‑Key for passive RC, op‑amp integrator for active).  
4. **Derive transfer function** using chosen topology.  
5. **Calculate component values**  
   - Start with standard values (e.g., \(R=10\,k\Omega\), \(C=10\,nF\)).  
   - Adjust to meet cut‑off or resonant frequency.  
6. **Simulate** (SPICE, MATLAB/Simulink).  
7. **Iterate**: tweak component values, re‑simulate.  
8. **Check stability** (phase margin > 45°, gain margin > 6 dB).  
9. **Prototype on breadboard**.  
10. **Measure** (oscilloscope, LCR meter).  
11. **Validate against specs**.  
12. **Finalize PCB layout** (minimize trace inductance, proper grounding).

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES & TROUBLESHOOTING

| # | Mistake | Symptom | Corrective Action |
|---|---------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Missing decoupling capacitors** | Oscilloscope shows high‑frequency noise on supply rails, op‑amp oscillation | Add 0.1 µF ceramic + 10 µF electrolytic close to each power pin |
| 2 | **Incorrect resistor tolerance** | Filter cutoff deviates > 10 % from design | Use 1 % resistors for critical timing, verify with multimeter |
| 3 | **Polarity error on electrolytic** | Circuit does not power, LED stays off | Check orientation, reverse polarity leads to short |
| 4 | **Ground loop on breadboard** | Unexpected voltage offsets, noise | Use single‑point ground, keep ground traces short |
| 5 | **Overloading op‑amp output** | Output saturates at rail, no linear region | Add current‑limiting resistor or use rail‑to‑rail op‑amp |

## 6. VIVA & INTERVIEW TRAPS

| Question | Correct Answer |
|----------|----------------|
| 1 | *Explain the difference between a transfer function and a state‑space model.* | Transfer function describes input‑output relation in Laplace domain; state‑space gives internal state equations, useful for multi‑input/multi‑output and time‑domain simulation. |
| 2 | *Why does a first‑order RC low‑pass filter have a 3 dB point at \(f_c = 1/(2\pi RC)\)?* | At \(f_c\), magnitude \( |H(j\omega)| = 1/\sqrt{2} \) of DC gain, corresponding to –3 dB. |
| 3 | *What is the effect of ESR in a capacitor on a power‑supply decoupling network?* | ESR adds series resistance, limiting current ripple and increasing power dissipation; high ESR degrades filtering at high frequencies. |
| 4 | *Derive the quality factor \(Q\) for a series RLC circuit.* | \( Q = \frac{1}{R}\sqrt{\frac{L}{C}} = \frac{f_0}{\Delta f} \), where \(f_0 = 1/(2\pi\sqrt{LC})\). |
| 5 | *Explain why an op‑amp with a single supply cannot output a negative voltage.* | With single supply, the op‑amp’s common‑mode input range and output swing are limited to 0 V to V_DD; negative output would require negative rail. Use rail‑to‑rail op‑amp or bias the input. |
