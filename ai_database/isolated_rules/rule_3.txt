
## 1. CORE FORMULAS & MATHEMATICS

### BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor)
- **DC Biasing Equations**:
  - \( I_C = \beta I_B \)
  - \( I_E = I_C + I_B \)
  - \( V_{BE} \approx 0.7V \) (Si), \( 0.3V \) (Ge)
  - \( V_{CE} = V_{CC} - I_C R_C - I_E R_E \) (Common Emitter)
- **Current Gain**:
  - \( \beta = \frac{I_C}{I_B} \)
  - \( \alpha = \frac{I_C}{I_E} \approx 1 - \frac{1}{\beta} \)
- **Early Effect**:
  - \( I_C = \beta I_B (1 + \frac{V_{CE}}{V_A}) \)
  - \( V_A \): Early voltage (typically 50–150V)
- **Transconductance**:
  - \( g_m = \frac{I_C}{V_T} \) (where \( V_T = \frac{kT}{q} \approx 26mV \) at 300K)
- **Boundary Conditions**:
  - **Cutoff**: \( V_{BE} < 0.7V \), \( I_C \approx 0 \)
  - **Active**: \( V_{BE} \approx 0.7V \), \( V_{CE} > V_{BE} \)
  - **Saturation**: \( V_{CE} \leq V_{BE} \)

### FET (Field Effect Transistor)
- **JFET (Junction FET)**:
  - Shockley Equation: \( I_D = I_{DSS} \left(1 - \frac{V_{GS}}{V_P}\right)^2 \)
  - \( I_{DSS} \): Saturation drain current (at \( V_{GS} = 0 \))
  - \( V_P \): Pinch-off voltage (negative for N-channel)
- **MOSFET (Metal-Oxide Semiconductor FET)**:
  - **Saturation Region**: \( I_D = \frac{1}{2} \mu_n C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} (V_{GS} - V_{th})^2 (1 + \lambda V_{DS}) \)
  - **Triode Region**: \( I_D = \mu_n C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} \left[ (V_{GS} - V_{th}) V_{DS} - \frac{V_{DS}^2}{2} \right] \)
  - \( V_{th} \): Threshold voltage (typically 0.7–3V for N-channel)
  - \( \mu_n C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} \): Process transconductance parameter (\( K_n \))
- **Transconductance**:
  - \( g_m = \frac{\partial I_D}{\partial V_{GS}} \bigg|_{V_{DS}=const} \)
  - For MOSFET in saturation: \( g_m = \sqrt{2 \mu_n C_{ox} \frac{W}{L} I_D} \)

## 2. DESIGN ORDER OF OPERATIONS

### BJT Common Emitter Amplifier:
1. **Select Operating Point**: Choose \( I_C \), \( V_{CE} \), \( V_{CEQ} \)
2. **Determine \( \beta \)**: Use datasheet or assume \( \beta = 100–200 \)
3. **Calculate Base Current**: \( I_B = \frac{I_C}{\beta} \)
4. **Choose \( R_C \)**: \( R_C = \frac{V_{CC} - V_{CEQ}}{I_C} \)
5. **Choose \( R_E \)**: \( R_E = \frac{V_{EQ}}{I_E} \) (stability factor \( S \geq 10 \))
6. **Calculate \( R_1, R_2 \)**:
   - \( R_2 = \frac{R_1 \beta}{10} \)
   - \( R_1 = \frac{(V_{CC} - V_{BE}) \beta}{I_B + \frac{V_{CC} - V_{BE}}{R_2}} \)
7. **Verify \( V_{CE} \)**: Ensure \( V_{CE} > V_{CE(sat)} \)

### MOSFET Common Source Amplifier:
1. **Select \( V_{GS} \) and \( I_D \)**: Based on \( V_{th} \) and \( K_n \)
2. **Choose \( R_D \)**: \( R_D = \frac{V_{DD} - V_{DSQ}}{I_D} \)
3. **Choose \( R_S \)**: \( R_S = \frac{V_{GSQ} - V_{th}}{I_D} \)
4. **Calculate \( R_G \)**: \( R_G = \frac{V_{GG}}{I_{in(max)}} \) (input resistance)
5. **Check \( g_m \)**: \( g_m = \frac{2 I_D}{V_{GSQ} - V_{th}} \)
6. **Verify \( V_{DS} \)**: Ensure \( V_{DS} > V_{GS} - V_{th} \) (saturation)

## 3. STANDARD VALUES & PARAMETERS

### Supply Voltages:
- **Digital Circuits**: 3.3V, 5V
- **Analog/Power Circuits**: 12V, 15V, ±12V (op-amps)
- **High-Voltage Applications**: 24V, 48V

### Component Values:
- **Resistors**: 1kΩ, 4.7kΩ, 10kΩ, 100kΩ (1/4W standard)
- **Capacitors**:
  - Coupling: 1μF, 10μF (electrolytic)
  - Bypass: 100μF (electrolytic)
  - Decoupling: 0.1μF (ceramic)
- **Transistors**:
  - BJT: 2N2222 (NPN), 2N2907 (PNP), BC547 (low-noise)
  - MOSFET: 2N7000 (N-channel), IRF540 (N-channel power), BS170 (N-channel)

### Operating Ranges:
- **BJT**:
  - \( \beta \): 100–400
  - \( V_{CEO} \): 30–40V
  - \( I_C(max) \): 10–100mA
- **FET**:
  - \( V_{th} \): 0.7–3V (N-channel)
  - \( I_{DSS} \): 1–10mA (JFET), 1–100A (power MOSFET)
  - \( f_T \): 1MHz–100MHz

## 4. PRACTICAL COMPONENT SELECTION RULES

- **Capacitors**:
  - **Electrolytic**: Coupling/bypass (≥1μF, polarized)
  - **Ceramic**: High-frequency decoupling (≤1μF, non-polarized)
  - **Film**: Precision filtering (≤100nF, low tolerance)
- **Resistors**:
  - **1/4W**: Signal-level circuits (≤5mA)
  - **1/2W**: Medium power (≤10mA)
  - **1W**: Power amplifiers (≥10mA)
- **Transistors**:
  - **BJT**: Use 2N2222/BC547 for general-purpose amplification
  - **MOSFET**: Use 2N7000 for switching, IRF540 for power applications
- **ICs**:
  - Op-Amps: LM741 (general-purpose), TL071 (low-noise)
  - Comparators: LM393 (dual, open-collector)

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES & TROUBLESHOOTING

1. **Incorrect Biasing**:
   - **Symptom**: Transistor in cutoff/saturation
   - **Fix**: Recalculate \( R_1, R_2 \) to ensure \( V_{BE} \approx 0.7V \)
2. **Wrong Resistor Values**:
   - **Symptom**: Overheating or no amplification
   - **Fix**: Use \( R_C = \frac{V_{CC} - V_{CE}}{I_C} \) and verify power ratings
3. **Ignoring Early Effect**:
   - **Symptom**: Output distortion at high \( V_{CE} \)
   - **Fix**: Add emitter degeneration (\( R_E \)) to stabilize \( I_C \)
4. **Improper Grounding**:
   - **Symptom**: Noise in output
   - **Fix**: Use star grounding; separate analog/digital grounds
5. **Wrong Capacitor Type**:
   - **Symptom**: Signal clipping or AC coupling failure
   - **Fix**: Use electrolytic for DC blocking; ceramic for high-frequency paths

## 6. VIVA & INTERVIEW TRAPS

1. **Q**: Why is a BJT called a current-controlled device?
   - **A**: Base current \( I_B \) controls collector current \( I_C \) via \( \beta \).

2. **Q**: What is the Early effect, and how does it impact \( I_C \)?
   - **A**: Base-width modulation reduces \( \beta \) with increasing \( V_{CE} \); modeled by \( V_A \).

3. **Q**: How do you stabilize the operating point against \( \beta \) variations?
   - **A**: Use emitter resistor \( R_E \) (emitter degeneration) to reduce \( S = \frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta \beta} \).

4. **Q**: Differentiate between common emitter and common source configurations.
   - **A**: BJT CE: High gain, 180° phase shift; MOSFET CS: High gain, 180° phase shift, high input impedance.

5. **Q**: What causes thermal runaway in a BJT?
   - **A**: Increased \( I_C \) raises \( T_j \), reducing \( V_{BE} \), leading to further \( I_C \) rise. Mitigated via \( R_E \) or negative feedback.
