## 1. CORE FORMULAS & MATHEMATICS
- **Ohm’s Law**  
  \(V = I \cdot R\)  
  \(I = \frac{V}{R}\)  
  \(R = \frac{V}{I}\)
- **Power**  
  \(P = V \cdot I\)  
  \(P = I^2 \cdot R\)  
  \(P = \frac{V^2}{R}\)
- **Voltage Divider**  
  \(V_{out} = V_{in} \cdot \frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2}\)
- **Capacitive Reactance**  
  \(X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi f C}\)
- **Inductive Reactance**  
  \(X_L = 2\pi f L\)
- **Resonant Frequency (LC)**  
  \(f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{LC}}\)
- **Quality Factor (Q)**  
  \(Q = \frac{f_0}{\Delta f}\) or \(Q = \frac{X_L}{R}\) (series)  
  \(Q = \frac{R}{X_L}\) (parallel)
- **Gain (Voltage)**  
  \(A_v = \frac{V_{out}}{V_{in}}\)  
  For op‑amp inverting: \(A_v = -\frac{R_f}{R_{in}}\)  
  For non‑inverting: \(A_v = 1 + \frac{R_f}{R_{in}}\)
- **Gain (Current)**  
  \(A_i = \frac{I_{out}}{I_{in}}\)
- **Efficiency (DC‑DC)**  
  \(\eta = \frac{P_{out}}{P_{in}} \times 100\%\)
- **Ripple Voltage (Capacitor)**  
  \(\Delta V_{ripple} = \frac{I_{load}}{f \cdot C}\)
- **Thermal Resistance (Power Dissipation)**  
  \(T_j = T_a + \theta_{JA} \cdot P_{diss}\)
- **Signal‑to‑Noise Ratio (SNR)**  
  \(SNR_{dB} = 20 \log_{10}\left(\frac{V_{signal}}{V_{noise}}\right)\)

## 2. DESIGN PROCEDURE (Step‑by‑Step)
1. **Define Functional Requirements**  
   - Input/output voltage and current ranges  
   - Load type (resistive, capacitive, inductive)  
   - Switching frequency (if applicable)  
   - Desired efficiency, ripple, noise specifications  
2. **Select Topology**  
   - Linear regulator, buck, boost, buck‑boost, flyback, etc.  
   - Consider component count, cost, size, EMI.  
3. **Calculate Key Parameters**  
   - Output voltage/current using equations above.  
   - Switching frequency (trade‑off between size and losses).  
   - Inductor value: \(L = \frac{V_{in} - V_{out}}{\Delta I_L \cdot f}\)  
   - Capacitor value: \(C = \frac{\Delta I_L}{8 f \Delta V_{out}}\) (buck)  
   - Duty cycle: \(D = \frac{V_{out}}{V_{in}}\) (ideal).  
4. **Select Core Components**  
   - Power MOSFET/IGBT: voltage, current rating, Rds(on).  
   - Diode: Schottky or synchronous rectifier.  
   - Feedback network: resistors for reference voltage.  
   - Control IC or microcontroller (if required).  
5. **Thermal Analysis**  
   - Compute conduction and switching losses.  
   - Verify junction temperature with \(\theta_{JA}\).  
6. **EMI/ESD Protection**  
   - Add input/output filtering, TVS diodes, snubber networks.  
7. **PCB Layout Planning**  
   - Power plane, ground plane, trace width calculations.  
   - Minimize loop area for high‑frequency currents.  
8. **Prototype and Test**  
   - Build on breadboard or low‑cost PCB.  
   - Measure voltage, ripple, efficiency, thermal.  
9. **Iterate Design**  
   - Adjust component values, layout, or topology based on test data.  
10. **Finalize Documentation**  
    - Schematics, BOM, Gerber files, test reports.

## 4. COMPONENT SELECTION THRESHOLDS
- **Resistors**  
  - Power rating ≥ 1.5 × maximum dissipated power.  
  - Voltage derating: 80 % of maximum rated voltage.  
  - Tolerance: 1 % for precision, 5 % for general use.  
- **Capacitors**  
  - ESR ≤ 0.1 Ω for high‑frequency decoupling.  
  - Voltage derating: 70 % of rated voltage.  
  - Temperature coefficient: X7R or C0G for stability.  
- **Inductors**  
  - Current rating ≥ 1.5 × maximum DC current.  
  - Saturation current: >1.2 × max.  
  - DCR ≤ 0.1 Ω for low loss.  
- **MOSFETs**  
  - Drain‑source voltage ≥ 1.5 × maximum Vds.  
  - Continuous drain current ≥ 1.5 × max load.  
  - Rds(on) ≤ 0.05 Ω for low conduction loss.  
- **Diodes**  
  - Peak reverse voltage ≥ 1.5 × max.  
  - Forward current ≥ 1.5 × max.  
  - Schottky preferred for low drop.  
- **ICs**  
  - Supply voltage ±10 % of rated range.  
  - Temperature rating: -40 °C to +85 °C for student boards.  
  - Derating: 80 % of absolute maximum.

## 5. BREADBOARD & PCB TROUBLESHOOTING
| Symptom | Probable Cause | Test Method | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-------------|-----|
| No output voltage | Power supply not connected or shorted | Check input voltage with DMM | Re‑wire power rail, replace damaged trace |
| Excessive ripple | Undersized output capacitor or high ESR | Measure ripple with oscilloscope | Replace capacitor with lower ESR, increase capacitance |
| Overheating MOSFET | Incorrect gate drive or missing gate resistor | Measure gate voltage, check temperature | Add proper gate driver, increase gate resistor, improve heat sinking |
| Oscillation in output | Improper feedback loop or missing compensation | Observe output with oscilloscope | Add compensation capacitor, adjust feedback resistors |
| No load current | Faulty switching device or wrong orientation | Check continuity of MOSFET channel, verify diode orientation | Replace faulty MOSFET/diode, correct orientation |

## 6. VIVA & INTERVIEW TRAPS
1. **Explain why a Schottky diode is preferred over a standard silicon diode in a buck converter.**  
   *Answer:* Lower forward voltage drop reduces conduction loss and increases efficiency, especially at high switching frequencies.  
2. **Derive the formula for the resonant frequency of an LC filter used as a low‑pass output filter.**  
   *Answer:* \(f_0 = \frac{1}{2\pi\sqrt{LC}}\) from solving the differential equation of an LC circuit.  
3. **What is the impact of ESR on the output ripple of a switching regulator?**  
   *Answer:* Higher ESR increases ripple voltage (\(\Delta V = I_{load} \times ESR\)); low ESR capacitors reduce ripple.  
4. **Why must the MOSFET’s drain‑source voltage rating be at least 1.5 × the maximum input voltage?**  
   *Answer:* To provide margin against voltage spikes and ensure reliability under worst‑case conditions.  
5. **Describe how to calculate the required gate resistor value for a MOSFET in a 40 kHz buck converter.**  
   *Answer:* Use \(R_g = \frac{V_{drive} - V_{th}}{I_{gate}}\) with \(I_{gate}\) chosen to limit gate charge rise time to <10 % of switching period, ensuring proper turn‑on/off timing.
